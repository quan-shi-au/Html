Overview

You wouldn't leave your car unlocked or your front door open while you went out for the day, as such recklessness would significantly increase the risk of theft. Surprisingly, although most Australians would recognise the sense in ensuring that their material possessions are protected against unauthorised entry, many do not take similar precautions in order to secure their virtual information. This oversight results in around 20% of people being a victim of some form of identity theft each year.

These days it's not just credit card details that can be used fraudulently: bank details, passport information, social security identification, driving licence data and even your email address can all be misappropriated by skilled identity fraudsters and used in illegal activity. Unfortunately without the right security measures, anyone can be open to identity theft; you don't have to use a credit card or undertake some other form of online transaction in order to be at risk. If your private information is stored anywhere at all, there is potential for it to be stolen and used inappropriately. Our comprehensive identity theft prevention package includes a premium internet security suite, a high security desktop, a “watch and alert” service, insurance and remediation. What is Identity Theft Protection

Overview Summary

Identity theft protection presents a number of challenges: one of the most complex problems is the variety of different ways in which data can be stolen. From significant breaches in the security systems of major data handling organisations to inadvertently responding to a “phishing” email, there are numerous methods that unscrupulous virtual criminals use to get hold of your personal information. Combating their tactics requires a multi-faceted approach which both minimises the risk of your valuable data being taken and also includes a rapid, effective system for reclaiming anything which does go astray.

Protection at every stage

Identity theft protection involves taking action to secure your data at every stage. It also includes practical, real life measures you can adopt to reduce risk as well as ensuring that your virtual transactions and virtual data storage is as secure as possible. Monitoring your personal information use is always essential, so that any untoward activity can be swiftly identified and investigated. By carrying out continual information sweeps on how your information is being used, it becomes easier to spot suspicious activities. On-going monitoring and security are the hallmarks of effective identity theft protection.

Rapid, effective response

When an incident occurs, for example your purse containing cards and a driving licence is stolen, prompt implementation of protective measures minimise the risk of the data being used fraudulently. Should the worst happen and the information is passed onwards, identity theft protection includes remedial action to limit the amount of damage caused. Insurance is included as part of an identity theft package, enabling people who've been a victim of theft to make a claim for any losses they have incurred as a result.

Security for you and your family

Identity theft protection can be extended to cover transactions on any device, as well as include regular searches to make sure your personal information isn't being used illicitly. This ensures that even if you're not aware that your security has been compromised, you can be confident that protracted misuse of your details will be quickly discovered and resolved. If you're keen to enable the people you care about to go about their daily lives confident that their personal information isn't being used illegally, identity theft protection is essential.
